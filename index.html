Introduction

Homework has been a traditional part of education for decades, but growing evidence suggests it may be doing more harm than good. Students today face increasing pressure, stress, and burnout from excessive homework assignments that extend the school day well into evenings and weekends.

This presentation argues that homework should be banned to improve student mental health, promote better learning outcomes, and allow students to develop holistically beyond academics.

Supporting Evidence

Research Findings

  • Studies show homework has minimal academic benefits for elementary and middle school students
  • Excessive homework is linked to increased stress, sleep deprivation, and mental health issues
  • Countries with less homework (like Finland) often outperform those with heavy homework loads
  • Quality instruction during school hours is more effective than homework for learning retention

What Schools Can Do Instead

  • Focus on quality, engaging lessons during school hours
  • Provide optional enrichment activities for interested students
  • Encourage reading for pleasure and personal interest
  • Support project-based learning that students can complete in class
  • Promote extracurricular activities and hobbies
  • Respect students' time outside of school for rest and personal growth

Conclusion

Banning homework is essential for protecting student mental health, promoting balanced lives, and creating more effective learning environments. By eliminating homework, we allow students to be well-rested, engaged learners during school hours while having time for family, hobbies, and personal growth outside of academics.

It is time for schools to recognize that quality education happens during school hours and that students deserve their evenings and weekends free from academic work.
